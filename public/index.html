Featured Fosters

Rose

Rose is a LOVELY senior Aussie girl that was rescued off the euthanasia list in the nick-of-time! She is approximately 9-10 years old and was in rough medical shape when she first came to us (emaciation, hairloss, bacterial infections, etc), but her amazing foster parents have gone above and beyond to get her in tip-top shape! She is now fully-vetted and actively searching for her forever home!

Turner

This sweet, gentle soul is an approximately 10-year-old blue merle male we've named Turner. He was picked up as a stray in Corsicana, TX and was never reclaimed... which is a terrible shame, as Turner was clearly a beloved pet once before. He is already neutered, and knows how to sit, shake (with both paws), lay down, and roll over. He is totally house-broken, has wonderful manners, and walks beautifully on a leash. He also has evidence of a prior hip surgery, something that is very expensive! We wish Turner could tell us his story so we could track down the person who put so much time and love into him.

Todd

This sweet boy is housebroken, crate-trained, and leash-trained. He absolutely adores people! He is a true velcro-dog with a very solid, stable temperament. He has met, interacted, and played with countless other dogs and behaves famously with all of them... with the exception of only one. We believe this difference of opinion stemmed from both of them falling into the same position within the pack and Todd feels there is room for only one supreme shadow/snuggler!

Billy

Meet Billy – a seven year old Blue Merle Male with a lot of love to give. He is great with all people of all sizes (tall booming gregarious men, newborns, children running around, and he's even toddler tested - he enjoyed being petted), dogs, and cats. He came into rescue because his previous owner said he wasn't good with chickens – his foster family took him to a farm and Billy could not have cared less about any of the farm animals – including chickens!

Duncan

Duncan does well with other dogs of similar size that like to play and run. He enjoys a good game of fetch but is still learning the benefits of bringing it back every time. He does not have any tolerance for the feline persuasion, so a home without cats is required. However, he does do very well with older children that cannot accidentally be knocked over.

Scotch

Scotch is super smart.... almost "scary" smart! He catches onto everything only after 1 or 2 tries, and learns the household routine VERY quickly. The one who adopts Scotch should enjoy training, as he thrives off being able to think and problem-solve, and enjoys both treat and toy rewards.

Benefits to Fostering

Fostering means bringing in a "homeless" animal with the goal of nurturing them as part of your daily existance until they are able to find their permanent furrever family. Fostering is a crucial part of the animal rescue world. It can be an amazing experience not just for he animal, but for you too.

  • Foster dogs learn they are loved
  • You're directly saving a life
  • Teaches socialization with kids, pets and home life
  • Flexibilty for a few days to months or even to adoption
  • Part of their journey to finding their furrever family
